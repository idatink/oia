Logo motion

A mark that speaks

OIA is direct conversation, so the mark behaves like a participant, not a decoration. In the speech bubble the avatar carries six behavioral states, each mapped to a moment in the patient journey. Motion is always slow, small, and calm — never louder than the conversation itself. Each state ships as its own file in logos/states/.

Idle

A 2% breath over 6.4s. Present, alive, unobtrusive — the default in the chat header and thread avatar.

Listening

The patient's sliver gently swells while they type or record — their voice literally grows in the mark.

Speaking

The arcs open and close toward each other — Oia is composing or delivering a message.

Thinking

Three dots pulse between the arcs — SmartMatch is matching, curation is underway, or negotiation is in progress.

Confirming

The eclipse completes: the arcs close into a full circle as an offer is accepted or a booking confirmed. Plays once in product, then holds. (Looped here for preview.)

Still

Deliberately motionless — during consent prompts, the sensitive-imagery gate, and medical escalations. Stillness signals seriousness.

  • Small and slow. Scale never exceeds 2%; positional shifts never exceed 5% of the mark's width; cycles run 3s or slower.
  • Motion respects the moment. During the sensitive-imagery gate, consent prompts, and medical escalations, the mark holds still — animation never plays over serious moments.
  • One state at a time, driven by the conversation. The orchestrator sets the avatar state (idle → listening → thinking → speaking → confirming); states never blend or flicker between turns.
  • Honors prefers-reduced-motion. Product surfaces substitute oia-state-still.svg automatically.
  • Print and small sizes are static. Below 32px, and in any print or document context, use oia-monogram.svg.
Do's and don'ts

How to apply our brand

Do
  • Keep clear space equal to the width of the "I" on all sides. Let the aura breathe around it.
  • Approved colorways only: Signature Terracotta arc + deep Primary sliver on Cream/glass, or Cream arc + Terracotta sliver on inverse surface. The logo is never black or ink.
  • Respect minimums: lockup 96px, wordmark 64px, monogram 24px.
  • Co-brand with care: partner-clinic logos at equal visual weight, separated by clear space — never merged, never inside the monogram.
Don't
  • Don't stretch, rotate, outline, add shadows, or recolor the logo — never render it in black or ink; don't alter animation timings or invent new motion states.
  • Don't set the wordmark in another typeface or in lowercase — OIA is uppercase by design.
  • Don't place the logo over photography or directly on the aura without a glass panel behind it.
  • Don't let third parties use the marks without written permission — clinic partners receive assets through their agreement.

Luminous depth

The Aura and the glass

The interface lives atop an Aura — three to four large radial gradients (Terracotta, Cream, muted Slate) that slowly pulsate and drift, mirroring biological rhythms. Glass panels float above it, suggesting clarity and transparency in medical data. You're watching it behind this page.

Glass surface

Panels

Semi-transparent Cream rgba(252,249,247,.65) with minimum 24px blur (32px standard). Sidebars blur 40px+ so clinical data never bleeds through.

Rim light

Precision edges

1px hairline borders in brightened surface color; top and left brighter than bottom and right, simulating light from the top-left.

Respect motion

Restraint

The aura drifts slowly — never flashes or pulses aggressively. Honors prefers-reduced-motion. Calm is the luxury; motion never competes with the conversation.

Typography

Prestige meets efficiency

EB Garamond carries editorial and headline moments with generous tracking (+2–5%). Hanken Grotesk is the functional workhorse — clean, geometric, a high-tech counterpoint. Use 500/600 weights for interactive labels so they hold against blurred glass.

Elevation & depth

Refractive layering

Hierarchy comes from light and refraction, never black drop-shadows. Ambient shadows are color-tinted — Terracotta in warm contexts, Slate in clinical ones.

L1 — Base. The pulsating Aura gradient background.
L2 — Panels. Glass at 65% opacity, 32px blur, rim light, soft tinted ambient shadow.
L3 — Interactions. Active cards and modals gain a white 10% inner glow and a secondary 1px border — closer to the light.
L4 — Floating. Tooltips and critical alerts: 90% opacity, sharper 8px blur, popping against the softer layers.
Shapes & components

Softly architectural

Curves are generous but controlled: 1rem corners on containers balance approachability with professional structure. Pill shapes are reserved for chips and tags — primary containers are never pills, so the UI stays precise, not bubbly.

Photography

Sun-warmed, real, consensual

Our photography lives in the same light as the palette: golden hour, terracotta warmth, honest skin with real texture. Calm, editorial, unhurried — people at ease in their own skin, photographed with respect. It sits behind glass panels, never fighting the aura. No clinical settings, no operating theatres, no plastic retouching.

The profile

Quiet confidence in natural light. Warm plaster backdrops echo Nia Cream; skin keeps its texture and glow.

The detail

Honest close-ups: pores, fine hairs, luminosity. Realness is the luxury — never airbrushed to plastic.

The ease

Bodies at ease in sun and air — recovery, travel, life resumed. Aspiration framed as wellbeing, never as a promised outcome.

  • Before/after imagery is never decorative. Hard rule Clinical results appear only inside the conversation, behind the sensitive-imagery gate, with verified patient consent on record. Never in marketing, headers, or social.
  • Consent is a prerequisite, not a caption. No consent record, no usage — anywhere, ever.
  • Show care, not procedures. Recovery suites, morning light, the concierge experience — not the surgery.
  • Warmth over gloss. Golden-hour light, terracotta tones, real skin texture. If it looks like a filter or a clinic ad, it's wrong.
  • Aspiration ≠ promise. Hard rule Editorial imagery must never be paired with procedure claims, outcomes copy, or pricing in a way that implies a guaranteed result or trivialises surgery (UK CAP/ASA).

Voice and tone

Considered. Reassuring. Honest.

The visual system may be technical, but Oia's voice never is. She is the friend with impeccable judgement who happens to know everyone — never a salesperson, never clinical, never gushing. Warm, plain, elegant sentences; the patient always in control.

"Amazing choice!! 😍 You'll look INCREDIBLE""That's a beautiful, natural result to aim for. Let me find surgeons who achieve exactly this."
"Book now — offer ends soon!""There's no rush. When you're ready, I'll set it up."
"That swelling is totally normal, don't worry.""I'm not able to advise on anything medical, but I've alerted your clinic and I'll chase them until they reply."

Hard compliance rules — every word, everywhere UK CAP/ASA

  • No guaranteed results — before/afters illustrate that patient's outcome, never a promise.
  • No trivialising surgery — it is significant and carries risk; copy never makes it sound casual.
  • No pressure, urgency, scarcity, or time-limited framing. Ever.
  • No medical advice — suitability and clinical judgement belong to surgeons; Oia relays, never advises.
  • All prices indicative until a founder-approved offer; all clinical imagery behind the consent gate.
